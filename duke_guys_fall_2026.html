Monongahela NF · ~5hr from DC, ~5.5hr from Philly · BEST overall pick

🍂 Two Windows — Pick Your Priority

September (Sep 15–22): New moon Sep 20. Nights 45–55°F — comfortable, no 30°F risk. Foliage not peak but still green/gold. Best if warmth matters.

October (Oct 15–22): New moon Oct 20 + peak fall foliage. Spectacular but nights can hit 30°F — need 20°F bags and full winter layers.

Why Dolly Sods

✅ Pros

  • Most Colorado-like terrain east of Mississippi
  • Open tundra — above-treeline feel
  • Best dark sky in mid-Atlantic (Bear Rocks)
  • Red Creek native brook trout
  • Dispersed camping — total freedom
  • Fall foliage is stunning at elevation

⚠️ Watch Out

  • 5–6hr drive from DC/Philly
  • Trails unmaintained, some route-finding
  • Weather changes fast at 4,200ft
  • Avoid Columbus Day weekend

Savage River State Forest, MD
Western Maryland · ~2.5hr from DC, ~3.5hr from Philly · Most geographically centered

🎯 Sophie's Pick for Logistics — Best Midpoint

Savage River is literally centered between DC and Philly along the Appalachians. No one has a terrible drive. 55,000 acres, 78-mile trail system, and the Savage River tailwater is one of the best wild trout fisheries on the entire East Coast — brown, brook, rainbow, and occasional cutthroat. This is an underrated gem.

Allegheny National Forest, PA
Northwest Pennsylvania · ~4hr from Philly, ~5hr from DC · Fills the northern gap

🍂 Best Window: Oct 1–15

Largest national forest in PA (513,000 acres). Sits geographically between Cherry Springs and Delaware Water Gap — the true northern anchor in this corridor. Tionesta Creek is excellent fishing. Less dramatic scenery than WV options but more remote and far less crowded.
