💡 Best money-saving moves

  • Round-trip CLT ↔ HND on AA — free bags both ways, same airline, 30–40 min to Shinjuku
  • 7-day JR Pass (~$330/person) covers all bullet trains + local JR lines
  • Book teamLab Borderless NOW — sells out months in advance (~$30/person)
  • Fly Apr 28 — Golden Week (Apr 29–May 5) overlaps. Book hotels EARLY — they sell out. Upside: you're IN Japan for the festivals and holiday energy.
  • Points play — JAL/ANA biz class is one of the best point redemptions (Chase Sapphire → JAL)

📱 Logistics

Getting AroundIC Card (Suica/Pasmo) — load ¥5,000, tap on/off every subway and bus. The only app you need is Google Maps (offline download Tokyo + Kyoto).
7-Day JR Pass~$330/person. Covers all Shinkansen + local JR lines. Do the math for your exact route — if doing open-jaw (one-way Tokyo→Kyoto) it may not break even.
Data / SIMBuy a pocket WiFi at the airport or get an eSIM before you leave. Do not rely on hotel WiFi.
CashJapan is still mostly cash for small places. 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards. Keep ¥20,000–30,000 on hand at all times.

🍜 Food Strategy

Daily eats (cheap + amazing)Convenience stores (7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart) — seriously. Onigiri, hot sandwiches, great coffee, cold noodles. Not a compromise. Ramen shops: $10. Conveyor sushi: $15–20.
Splurge budget: 2 dinnersOne omakase in Tokyo (~$120/person), one kaiseki in Kyoto (~$120–150/person). These are genuinely life-changing meals. Reserve 60 days ahead via Tableall, Ikyu, or Omakase app.

WeatherPerfect walking weather. Tokyo 60–72°F, Kyoto 58–70°F. Pack layers for evenings. Rain jacket useful — brief afternoon showers possible.
